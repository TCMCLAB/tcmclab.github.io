Research Profile

Dr. Tumpa Sadhukhan is a theoretical and computational chemist with extensive research experience in molecular quantum chemistry, computational materials chemistry, catalysis, excited state chemistry, and data driven materials design. Her research integrates density functional theory, wave function based methods, semiempirical approaches, fragment based quantum mechanical methods, high throughput computation, and emerging machine learning strategies to understand and design molecules and materials with functional relevance.

Her research vision focuses on computer aided design of new molecules and materials for sustainable technologies. Current directions include catalysis, toxic gas separation, photodynamic therapy, light matter interaction, and computational discovery of functional molecular and materials platforms.

Research Interests

  • Theoretical and computational physical chemistry
  • Density functional theory and quantum chemical methods
  • Design of novel molecules and functional materials
  • Light matter interaction and excited state processes
  • Catalysis for sustainable chemical transformations
  • Toxic gas separation and environmental materials
  • Photodynamic therapy and molecular photosensitizers
  • High throughput DFT and machine learning assisted materials discovery
  • Fragment based quantum mechanical modelling
  • Low dimensional materials, molecular assemblies, nanoclusters, and surfaces

Academic and Professional Background

Dr. Sadhukhan received her Ph.D. in Theoretical and Computational Chemistry from the Indian Institute of Technology Bombay, where her doctoral work received the Excellence in Ph.D. Thesis Work award. She subsequently held research positions at IIT Bombay, the Institute for Molecular Science in Japan, Indiana University Bloomington, and Northwestern University before joining SRM Institute of Science and Technology.

Her postdoctoral research involved internationally recognized programmes, including the Molecular Materials Design Laboratory at Indiana University, the Center for Molecular Quantum Transduction at Northwestern University, and the Advanced Materials for Energy Water Systems Center at Argonne and Northwestern University.

Research Experience

SRM Institute of Science and Technology

As Research Assistant Professor in the Department of Chemistry, Dr. Sadhukhan leads research activities in theoretical and computational materials chemistry. Her group applies quantum chemical calculations, atomistic modelling, and data driven approaches to address molecular and materials problems relevant to catalysis, gas separation, photophysics, and sustainable technologies.

Northwestern University, USA

Her research at Northwestern University involved computational studies of functionalized defects in low dimensional materials for heralded photon emission and the separation of heavy metal ions using two dimensional materials.

Indiana University, USA

At Indiana University, her research focused on electronic excited states and fluorescent properties of organic dye molecules and assemblies. She also worked on the Molecules in Molecules fragment based approach for open shell systems and metal organic frameworks as catalytic systems.

Institute for Molecular Science, Japan

Her research at the Institute for Molecular Science focused on mechanistic studies of hydrosilylation reactions on AuPd nanoclusters and supported AuPd clusters on oxide surfaces.

Indian Institute of Technology Bombay, India

At IIT Bombay, she worked on theoretical and computational chemistry problems involving quantum chemical modelling, reaction mechanisms, molecular properties, and chemically relevant open and closed shell systems.

Computational Expertise

Dr. Sadhukhan has experience with a broad range of theoretical and computational chemistry methods, including wave function based approaches, density functional theory, semiempirical methods, fragment based methods, excited state calculations, periodic electronic structure methods, and molecular property prediction.

Her computational toolkit includes quantum chemistry and materials modelling software such as Gaussian, ORCA, Q-Chem, and VASP, along with scripting and data handling using Tcsh and Python. She also uses visualization and analysis tools including GaussView, Materials Studio, VESTA, Chemcraft, VMD, ChemDraw, MATLAB, Origin, and related computational resources.

Teaching

At SRM Institute of Science and Technology, Dr. Sadhukhan has contributed to teaching in Quantum Chemistry and Molecular Spectroscopy, Chemistry, Environmental Science, and Universal Human Values. She has also served as a teaching assistant at IIT Bombay for courses in chemical and statistical thermodynamics, computational chemistry, chemistry and computers, symmetry in chemistry, and advanced quantum chemistry.

Group Vision

The TCMC Research Group aims to develop a strong computational chemistry platform for understanding molecular and materials phenomena from first principles. The group seeks to connect quantum chemical insight, molecular scale modelling, and predictive materials design to address challenges in catalysis, environmental chemistry, energy materials, molecular photophysics, and functional material discovery.
